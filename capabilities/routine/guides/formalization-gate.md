# Routine — formalization gate

A routine is the heaviest rung of the lightweight formalization ladder:

1. **Nothing / inline judgment** when the need genuinely differs every time.
2. **Recipe note** when a small agreed treatment is worth recording but there is no multi-step procedure.
3. **Skill or prompt** when the procedure must be a harness feature or lightweight reusable instruction.
4. **Routine** when the procedure repeats, carries enough sequencing that re-deriving it is waste or risk, and is harness-independent.

High variability or a real judgment call every time is the signal not to write a routine. A routine authored before its time becomes authoritative-looking overhead that rots.

A candidate that is one CLI call the capability already documents is not a routine. Point at the capability help or guide instead. A routine encodes a multi-step procedure; a capability documents how to reach a mechanism. The two should not describe the same action at the same altitude.
